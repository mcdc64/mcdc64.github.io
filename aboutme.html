What is this?

I am an undergraduate physics student. When I'm not scratching my head over electrostatics problems, or normalising quantum wavefunctions, I spend a lot of my spare time programming. This portfolio showcases examples of what I have created. My language of choice is usually Python. This is mainly because of the matplotlib module, which offers powerful ways to visualise physical simulations in a small number of lines. However, I can also write simulations in C#, Java, or C++ if the situation calls for it (all three executing faster than Python).

About Me

I first began programming at the age of 12, with the intention of writing mods for Minecraft. Thus Java was my introduction to the world of datatypes, objects, and algorithms. However, I came to appreciate the lack of boilerplate code required in Python. It is now the main language that I use to write small projects (such as those seen on this site). Other than programming, I also enjoy gaming, reading and astronomy. From a young age, my dream has been to go to space.
