About

I am a (recently graduated!) Maths and Physics student. In my spare time, I really enjoy programming. This portfolio showcases examples of what I have created. My language of choice is usually Python. This is mainly because it offers powerful ways to visualise physical simulations in a small number of lines. However, I can also write simulations in C#, Java, or C++ if the situation calls for it (all three executing faster than Python in certain cases). This is useful if the task at hand is computationally expensive.

I first began programming at the age of 12, with the intention of writing mods for Minecraft. Thus Java was my introduction to the world of datatypes, objects, and algorithms. However, I came to appreciate the lack of boilerplate code required in Python. It is now the main language that I use to write small projects (such as those seen on this site). Other than programming, I also enjoy gaming, reading and astronomy.

From a young age, my dream has been to go to space. In October 2022 I will begin a Master's degree in Space Engineering at Cranfield University. I enjoy looking at planets and deep-sky objects using a telescope. So on this site I also have some astrophotos that I have taken over the last number of years. They can be found on the "Astrophotos" page.
